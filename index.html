Let's talk about your breed.

CURLY COAT · MATS FAST

Goldendoodle

Curly coats don't shed — they tangle.
Without weekly professional brushing,
mats form in days. That means
a full shave-down. No more fluff.

Weekly Bath & Care keeps
your Goldendoodle fluffy.

We brush out every tangle, dry the coat
properly, and check for mats at the root
before they become a problem.

CURLY/WAVY · HIDDEN MATS

Labradoodle

Mats form where you never check.
Behind the ears. Under the legs.
Near the collar. Around the tail.
A groomer catches these. You don't.

SHORT COAT · SKIN FOLDS

French Bulldog

"Short hair means no grooming."
Dead wrong.

Skin folds trap bacteria and yeast.
They stink. You got used to it.
Your guests haven't.

We clean and dry every
fold, every visit.

Proper fold cleaning prevents infection
and keeps your home smelling normal.

DOUBLE COAT · HEAVY SHEDDER

Siberian Husky

Huskies blow their undercoat twice a year
and shed constantly. Without regular blowouts,
their undercoat traps moisture
and causes hot spots.

Vacuuming more is not
a grooming plan.

Professional deshedding and blowouts
every 1-2 weeks remove the loose undercoat
before it causes problems.

DOUBLE COAT · WATER DOG

Golden Retriever

Goldens love mud, water,
and rolling in everything.
Their double coat holds onto all of it
for weeks without proper care.

LONG SILKY COAT · TEAR STAINS

Shih Tzu

That beautiful coat mats incredibly fast.
Tear staining worsens without regular
face cleaning. Eyes, ears, and teeth
all need constant attention.

CURLY COAT · ALL SIZES

Poodle

Standard, Miniature, or Toy — the coat
is the same. Poodle hair grows continuously
and never stops. Mats form from the root.
By the time you see one, it's pulling on skin.

DOUBLE COAT · 365-DAY SHEDDER

German Shepherd

Shepherds shed every single day of the year.
Their dense undercoat traps dead hair
against the skin, causing itching
and constant irritation.

SILKY COAT · DENTAL ISSUES

Yorkshire Terrier

Yorkies have the worst dental problems
of almost any breed. Fine hair tangles
around ears and paws constantly.
Every visit includes teeth brushing.

FLAT FACE · SKIN FOLDS

Bulldogs & Pugs

Every wrinkle is a breeding ground
for bacteria. That musty smell isn't
"just how they are." It's build-up
that needs regular professional cleaning.

SILKY COAT · EAR PRONE

Cavalier King Charles

Those gorgeous long ears trap moisture
and wax like nothing else. Cavaliers are
one of the most ear-infection-prone breeds.
Regular ear cleaning is non-negotiable.

LONG EARS · DENSE COAT

Cocker Spaniel

Dense, wavy coat that picks up
every burr, leaf, and tangle outdoors.
Long ears need the same attention
as Cavaliers — moisture and wax
build up fast without regular cleaning.

THICK DOUBLE COAT · HEAVY SHED

Bernese Mountain Dog

Massive thick coat that sheds year-round.
Dead undercoat mats against the skin
in weeks without professional deshedding.
Too big for most home grooming setups.

DOUBLE COAT · ACTIVE BREED

Australian Shepherd

Aussies run, swim, hike, and roll
in everything. Their double coat collects
dirt, debris, and loose undercoat fast.
An active dog needs active grooming.

SILKY WHITE COAT · TEAR STAINS

Maltese

White coat shows every bit of staining.
Tear stains, urine stains, food stains —
all visible immediately. Without regular
bathing and face cleaning, they look
neglected fast.

LOW RIDER · BIG SHEDDER

Corgi

Small body, massive shedding problem.
Their low clearance means their belly
drags through wet grass, mud, and dirt.
Double coat needs regular deshedding
to stay healthy.

CURLY COAT · CONSTANT GROWTH

Bichon Frise

That fluffy cloud look takes serious work.
Bichon coats mat within days without
proper brushing. The coat grows non-stop
and tangles at the root just like Poodles.

SHORT COAT · NOSE FOR TROUBLE

Beagle

Beagles follow their nose into everything.
Short coat but heavy shedders with
oily skin that builds up fast. Their long
floppy ears trap moisture and wax —
prime breeding ground for infection.

SHORT COAT · SENSITIVE SKIN

Boxer

Boxers have extremely sensitive skin
prone to allergies, bumps, and irritation.
Regular bathing with proper products
and skin checks catch issues early —
before they become expensive vet visits.

MASSIVE DOUBLE COAT · CLOUD DOG

Samoyed

That beautiful white cloud coat is one
of the highest-maintenance in the dog world.
Without regular blowouts and deshedding,
the undercoat mats into felt against the skin.

LOW RIDER · THREE COAT TYPES

Dachshund

Smooth, wire, or long-haired — all three
need regular care. Their low body picks up
everything from the ground. Long-haired
Dachshunds mat fast behind the ears
and under the belly.

WIRE COAT · BEARD MAINTENANCE

Miniature Schnauzer

That iconic beard and eyebrows collect
food, water, and dirt constantly. Wire coats
need regular stripping or brushing.
Without it, the coat gets dull and matted.
